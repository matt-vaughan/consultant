My experience

I started in software before earning a degree in 2006 at Touchstone Software and, later, via aquisition, Phoenix Technologies. I had already taught myself how to program as a child, but was now able to use these skills to automate my daily workflow. I went to college at UMass Lowell where I earned a bachelor's of science in computer science and graduated with highest honors. I worked at IBM directly after college as a software engineer. I built user interfaces for Sterling Order Management, the order management system used by companies including Target, Best Buy, and Walmart. I was accepted for a Ph.D. at Tufts University and left IBM to begin pursuing a doctorate. For finacial reasons I had to reduce to part-time enrollment in my Ph.D. program and began working full-time at Salesforce as an associate member of techical staff (software engineer II.) Unfortunetly I had to withdraw from Tufts and quit Salesforce to seek treatment for a serious health concern. Recovered from my prolonged health struggle I now freelance. I assist individuals and small businesses with their various technical needs. I also tutor students in computer science and teach programming to intersted parties.
